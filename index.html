We build plans for both business situations

The biggest mistake agencies make is selling one generic package to everyone. We separate clients into two types and build the correct plan.

Type A: New Business (starting from zero)

A new business must build the foundation first, then launch marketing. Skipping the foundation causes wasted spend.

  • Needs: Branding + website + tracking + launch demand generation (ads + content).
  • Risk: launching without systems wastes cash fast.
  • Success definition: a working lead machine and a clear offer buyers understand.

Type B: Existing Business (already spending)

Existing businesses usually have activity, but the results are inconsistent because something is broken.

  • Needs: diagnose what is broken, fix tracking, restructure campaigns, improve conversion, scale what works.
  • Risk: money is spent but results are declining or unclear.
  • Success definition: stable lead volume and improving cost per lead month over month.

What ‚Äúunclear scope‚Äù looks like (why businesses get frustrated)

  • Deliverables are not counted (no one knows what is included).
  • Timelines are not defined (work drifts with no deadlines).
  • Ownership is unclear (client does not know who is responsible).
